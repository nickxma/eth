# Settlement & Finality

Settlement is the moment a payment becomes irreversible. The sender can't claw it back. The receiver can treat the funds as theirs. Everything before that moment is a promise.

In traditional finance, settlement and payment confirmation are different events separated by hours or days. On Ethereum, they converge.

## How Traditional Settlement Works

**ACH (Automated Clearing House)** processes payments in batches. A bank submits a file of transactions to the Federal Reserve or EPN, which nets the obligations and settles them against reserve accounts. Standard ACH settles next business day. Same-day ACH settles within hours but with dollar limits and cutoff times. Weekends and holidays pause the system entirely.

**Wire transfers (Fedwire)** settle in real time during business hours. They are irrevocable once processed. But Fedwire operates roughly 21.5 hours per business day and is closed on weekends and federal holidays. International wires route through correspondent banks, adding 1-5 business days and fees at each hop.

**SWIFT** is a messaging network, not a settlement system. It transmits payment instructions between banks. Actual settlement happens through correspondent banking relationships — a chain of intermediary accounts. A cross-border SWIFT payment typically involves 2-5 banks and takes 1-5 days. Each intermediary deducts fees and introduces delay.

**Card networks** authorize transactions in seconds but settle much later. When you tap your card, the merchant receives an authorization — a promise from the issuing bank. Actual settlement between the acquiring bank and issuing bank happens in 1-3 business days through the card network's clearing process. The merchant receives funds minus interchange fees, typically 1.5-3%.

## How Ethereum Settlement Works

A transaction on Ethereum L1 is included in a block approximately every 12 seconds. Once included, it is visible to the entire network. But inclusion is not finality.

Ethereum uses proof-of-stake consensus with a finality mechanism called Casper FFG. Validators vote on blocks in two rounds: attestation and justification. After two epochs (approximately 13 minutes), a block reaches economic finality. Reverting a finalized block would require destroying at least one-third of all staked ETH — over $10 billion at current values. This is a concrete economic cost an attacker must bear.

For most payment use cases, probabilistic confirmation after 1-2 blocks (12-24 seconds) provides sufficient assurance. The recipient can observe the transaction onchain and assess the reversion risk, which decreases exponentially with each subsequent block.

## L2 Settlement: Speed and Tradeoffs

Layer 2 networks offer faster confirmation but with a different finality model.

**Optimistic rollups** (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base) confirm transactions in under a second via a centralized sequencer. The sequencer orders transactions and provides a "soft confirmation." These confirmations are reliable for most payment scenarios — the sequencer has no economic incentive to reorder or exclude valid transactions. However, the transaction does not inherit Ethereum's full security until the rollup posts its data to L1 and the challenge period expires (currently 7 days for fraud proofs).

**ZK rollups** (zkSync, Scroll, Linea) also confirm quickly via a sequencer, but their finality path is different. Instead of a challenge window, ZK rollups generate a cryptographic proof that the state transition is valid. Once this proof is verified on L1, the transactions are final with Ethereum's full security. Proof generation currently takes minutes to hours, but the trend is toward real-time proving.

For a payment at a coffee shop, the sequencer's soft confirmation (sub-second) is sufficient. For a $10 million treasury transfer, an institution might wait for L1 finality.

## What This Means for Payments

| Property | ACH | SWIFT | Card Networks | Ethereum L1 | Ethereum L2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Confirmation | Same day - next day | 1-5 days | Seconds (auth only) | 12 seconds | Sub-second |
| Final settlement | 1-2 business days | 1-5 business days | 1-3 business days | ~13 minutes | Minutes to hours (proof dependent) |
| Availability | Business days | Business days | 24/7 (auth); business days (settlement) | 24/7/365 | 24/7/365 |
| Reversibility | Possible (60 days for consumer) | Difficult but possible | Chargebacks (120 days) | Irreversible at finality | Irreversible at L1 finality |

The 24/7 availability is underappreciated. Traditional payment systems shut down on weekends and holidays. A Friday evening payment doesn't settle until Monday — or Tuesday if Monday is a holiday. Ethereum processes blocks every 12 seconds regardless of calendar.

Irreversibility cuts both ways. Chargebacks protect consumers in card transactions. Onchain payments have no built-in reversal mechanism. Payment applications that need consumer protections must implement them at the application layer — through escrow contracts, dispute resolution protocols, or custodial intermediaries.

Settlement speed, availability, and finality make Ethereum competitive with the fastest traditional rails and superior to most. The remaining gap is cost and throughput, addressed by Layer 2 networks.
